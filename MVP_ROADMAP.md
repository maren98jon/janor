# MVP Roadmap

## Strategy

The product should be built in deliberate layers. The first release should already feel premium and coherent, but scope must stay disciplined.[cite:3][cite:34]

## MVP v1

### Goal

Deliver a complete and believable core experience for household food management.

### Included

- Mobile-first shell
- Bottom navigation
- Home screen
- Inventory management
- Expiration visibility
- Recipe management
- Recipe-to-inventory matching
- Weekly meal plan
- Shopping list generation
- Core empty/loading/error states
- Premium UX/UI system
- PWA installability baseline.[web:36]

### Excluded

- Barcode scanning
- OCR
- AI photo recognition
- Nutrition breakdown
- Third-party supermarket integrations
- Advanced analytics
- Push notifications

## v1.1

- Better search
- Favorites and recurring products
- Leftovers
- Better merge suggestions
- Quick-add intelligence

## v1.2

- Household collaboration refinement
- Shopping completion to inventory workflows
- Offline-first improvements
- Better recommendation ranking

## v2

- Barcode and OCR ingestion
- AI-assisted recipe suggestions
- AI-assisted fridge photo parsing
- Anti-waste weekly insights
- Cost and waste analytics

## Delivery priorities

When trade-offs appear, prioritize in this order:

1. Core UX quality
2. Flow completion
3. Visual polish
4. Data realism
5. Extra intelligence features

A smaller but extremely polished product is preferred over a broad but generic one.[cite:31][cite:32]
