# Product Vision

## Product definition

This product is a mobile-first household food management application built in Next.js as a Progressive Web App. Its mission is to help users decide what to cook, know what food is available at home, avoid duplicate purchases, track expirations, and reduce food waste through one unified workflow.[cite:1][web:13][web:15][web:17][web:36]

The product should not be treated as only a recipe app, only an inventory app, or only a grocery list app. It should be defined as an end-to-end home food operating system connecting pantry tracking, expiration management, meal planning, cooking, and shopping.[web:13][web:15][web:17][web:22]

## Core value proposition

The product should deliver four clear outcomes:

- Help the user know what is currently available at home.
- Help the user cook using available ingredients first.
- Help the user buy only what is needed.
- Help the user waste less food over time.[web:13][web:15][web:17]

## Product promise

The product should feel like a calm and intelligent kitchen companion. It should reduce decision fatigue, reduce waste, reduce repeated manual organization, and create trust through consistent small daily interactions.[web:13][web:15][web:22]

## Target users

### Primary users

- Individuals managing their own meals at home.
- Couples sharing fridge, pantry, shopping, and planning.
- Families coordinating meals and food stock across several categories.
- Users who care about reducing food waste and improving organization.

### Secondary users

- Fitness-oriented users who want more control over meal structure.
- Users who cook frequently and maintain a recipe collection.
- Shared-home users who need collaborative grocery and pantry management.[web:20][web:21][web:24]

## Product principles

- Mobile-first by default, desktop-capable by design.[web:36][web:37][web:45]
- Fast and low-friction for repetitive actions.
- Premium and natural rather than flashy.[web:53][web:54]
- Built around real household workflows rather than isolated features.
- Optimized for reuse of information: one input should power multiple outcomes.
- Designed to scale from MVP to richer intelligence features over time.[cite:28][cite:34]
