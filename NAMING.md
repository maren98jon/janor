# Naming

## Naming strategy

The name should feel premium, calm, memorable, and natural. It should avoid sounding generic, over-technical, or obviously AI-generated. It should also fit a product that sits between food, home, organization, and cooking.[cite:1]

## Naming directions

### Direction A: premium and minimal

- Larder
- Gather
- Vesto
- Savia
- Acre
- Grove

### Direction B: warm and home-centered

- Alacena
- Mesa
- Nido
- Miga
- Fogón
- Sazón

### Direction C: modern product brand

- Avora
- Viora
- Anera
- Noura
- Elio
- Velda

## Recommended shortlist

### 1. Savia

Why it works:
- Organic and elegant.
- Feels alive, fresh, and premium.
- Memorable in Spanish-speaking contexts.
- Works well with a calm visual system.

Risk:
- Slightly abstract, so brand narrative should explain it.

### 2. Alacena

Why it works:
- Extremely relevant to the product domain.
- Distinctive and ownable.
- Warm, familiar, and not childish.
- Strong semantic link to food storage and home.

Risk:
- More culturally anchored to Spanish than international names.

### 3. Larder

Why it works:
- Premium, short, and strongly linked to pantry storage.
- Mature and product-like.

Risk:
- Less natural in Spanish-speaking contexts.

### 4. Mesa

Why it works:
- Warm, human, food-centered, and simple.
- Easy to remember.

Risk:
- More lifestyle-oriented than inventory-oriented.

### 5. Gather

Why it works:
- Suggests bringing together recipes, ingredients, and people.
- Calm and premium.

Risk:
- More abstract and broader than the actual scope.

## Recommendation

If the brand should feel more emotional and elegant, choose **Savia**.

If the brand should feel more direct, useful, and semantically perfect in Spanish, choose **Alacena**.
